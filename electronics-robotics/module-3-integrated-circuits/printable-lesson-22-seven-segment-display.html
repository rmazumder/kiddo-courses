📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🔅 7
7-Segment Display
A component with 7 LED bars (segments a–g) arranged like a figure-8 to display any digit 0–9. Found in clocks and microwaves!
🔋 CA
Common Anode
All the + legs of the LEDs inside are joined together. You turn a segment ON by connecting its pin to GND (ground).
🔢 IC
7447 Decoder IC
A special chip that takes a 4-bit binary input and automatically lights up the right segments to display a digit — no manual wiring!
🔄 BCD
BCD (Binary Coded Decimal)
A way to represent digits 0–9 using 4 switches, each worth 8, 4, 2, or 1. Flip switches on/off to pick any number!

💡 My Activity Today
FULL 7447 DECODER CIRCUIT: +VCC ➞➼➼ 7447 Pin 16 (VCC) GND ➞➼➼ 7447 Pin 8 (GND) +VCC ➞➼➼ Pin 3 (LT), Pin 4 (BI), Pin 5 (RBI) 4 Push buttons ➞ Pins 7(A), 1(B), 2(C), 6(D) [with 10k pull-down resistors] 7447 Out pins ➞ 330Ω resistors ➞ 7-segment display segments Pin 13 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg a | Pin 12 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg b Pin 11 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg c | Pin 10 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg d Pin 9 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg e | Pin 15 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg f Pin 14 ➞ 330Ω ➞ seg g Display Common Anode (CA) ➞ +VCC
The 7447 outputs LOW voltage (~0V) to turn a segment ON (common-anode). Outputs go HIGH (~5V) to turn a segment OFF.
✅ Wiring Steps — Check Each One:
  1. 1 Place the 7447 IC on the breadboard with the notch facing up (toward you).
  2. 2 Connect Pin 16 to (+) rail and Pin 8 to (−) rail for power and ground.
  3. 3 Tie Pins 3, 4, and 5 to (+) rail — this enables the chip for normal operation.
  4. 4 Wire 4 push buttons (A, B, C, D) to pins 7, 1, 2, 6 with 10kΩ pull-down resistors.
  5. 5 Connect each output pin (13, 12, 11, 10, 9, 15, 14) through a 330Ω resistor to the correct segment on the display.
  6. 6 Connect the display's Common Anode (CA) pin to (+) rail.

🔧 Troubleshooting Quick Guide
ProblemQuick Fix
Display shows nothingCheck CA to +VCC and resistors to GND
Wrong segments light upMatch 7447 output pins to correct segments
Garbled numbersRecheck BCD inputs A, B, C, D order
Display very dimTry 220Ω resistors instead of 330Ω
Segment never lightsTest LED continuity with Wand
Nothing works at allPins 3, 4, 5 must be tied to +VCC
