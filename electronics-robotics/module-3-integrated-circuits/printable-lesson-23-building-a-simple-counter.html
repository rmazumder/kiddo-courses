📚 Key Words — Learn These!
⏱ CLK
Clock Signal
A repeating pulse that flips HIGH-LOW-HIGH-LOW over and over. It is the heartbeat that tells the counter when to step forward.
🔢 4017
Decade Counter
A chip that counts from 0 to 9 (ten steps). Each clock pulse moves it to the next output. After 9 it loops back to 0!
👥 Q0–Q9
Output (Q0–Q9)
The ten numbered pins on the 4017. Only one is HIGH at a time. When it goes HIGH it lights up the LED wired to that pin.
🔃 CO
Carry Out
A signal that goes HIGH after a full 0–9 cycle. You can use it to chain two 4017 chips together and count even higher!

🔨 The 4017 Decade Counter Pinout
⏰ 555 Timer (Clock Source)
Astable mode — same as Lesson 18! +9V → [R1 1k] → Pin 7 [R2 10k] → Pin 6+2 [C1 10uF] → GND Pin 8=VCC Pin 1=GND Pin 4=+9V Pin 5=0.01uF→GND Pin 3=OUTPUT → 4017 Pin 14
With R1=1k, R2=10k, C1=10uF:
~7 pulses per second — fast enough to see the chase, slow enough to follow!
🔢 4017 Decade Counter
Notch (top) +--------+ Q5→| 1 16|←VCC (+9V) Q1→| 2 15|←Reset (→GND) Q0→| 3 14|←Clock Input Q2→| 4 13|←Clock En (→GND) Q6→| 5 12|←Carry Out Q7→| 6 11|←Q9 Q3→| 7 10|←Q4 GND→| 8 9|←Q8 +--------+ Pin 13 + Pin 15 → GND always!
The output pins are NOT in order (Q0=Pin3, Q1=Pin2, Q2=Pin4…). Always double-check the pin map before wiring your LEDs!

💡 My Activity Today — Build the LED Chaser!
555 CLOCK (astable) 4017 COUNTER + 10 LEDs +9V +9V | | [R1 1k] 4017 Pin 16 (VCC) | | +--Pin 7 Pin 3 (OUT)----Pin 14 (CLK) | Pin 13 (CE) ---GND [R2 10k] Pin 15 (RST)---GND | Pin 8 (GND)---GND +--Pin 6 + Pin 2 | Each output pin: [C1 10uF] Q-pin --> [330ohm] --> LED(+) --> LED(-) --> GND | GND Pin 5 -- [0.01uF] -- GND
Pin 13 (Clock Enable) and Pin 15 (Reset) MUST both go to GND. If either is left floating, the counter will not work!
  1. 1Build the 555 astable clock: R1=1k, R2=10k, C1=10uF. Test it with one LED on Pin 3 first.
  2. 2Place the 4017 on the breadboard. Connect Pin 16 to +9V rail and Pin 8 to GND rail.
  3. 3Connect 4017 Pin 13 and Pin 15 to the GND rail.
  4. 4Connect 555 Pin 3 (output) to 4017 Pin 14 (clock input).
  5. 5Wire each of the 10 output pins through a 330Ω resistor to an LED, then LED to GND.
  6. 6Connect the battery and watch the LEDs chase one by one!

🔬 4 Experiments You Did Today!
A
Speed it Up!
Swap R2 from 10k to 1k. Keep C1=10uF.
The LEDs chase faster — a smaller R2 means a higher clock frequency!
B
Slow Motion Chase
Swap R2=47k and C1=47uF.
The counter steps so slowly you can count each tick out loud — about once every 3 seconds!
C
Wand Check — Find the HIGH Pin!
Set the multimeter to DC Voltage. Touch each output pin while circuit runs slowly.
One pin reads ~9V (HIGH). All others read ~0V (LOW). Watch it move!
D
5-LED Loop!
Connect Q5 (Pin 1) to the Reset pin (Pin 15) with a jumper wire.
The counter resets after 5 steps — only 5 LEDs chase now. You changed the loop length!
