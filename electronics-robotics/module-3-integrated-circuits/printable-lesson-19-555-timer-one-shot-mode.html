📚 Key Words — Learn These!
⏲ 1x
Monostable
"Mono" = one, "stable" = steady. The 555 fires ONE pulse when triggered, stays on for a set time, then returns to its resting state until triggered again.
💡 T
Pulse Duration
How long the output stays HIGH (LED on). Controlled by resistor R and capacitor C using the formula T = 1.1 × R × C.
🔌 2
Trigger (Pin 2)
The 555's start button. When Pin 2 drops below 1/3 of VCC (by pressing the push button), the one-shot pulse begins!
△ C
Threshold (Pin 6)
The timer's finish line. When the capacitor charges up to 2/3 of VCC, Pin 6 detects it and tells the 555 — time's up, turn off!

🔬 The Magic Timing Formula
⏱ Formula: T = 1.1 × R × C
T = time ON (in seconds) R = resistor (in Ohms) C = capacitor (in Farads) Bigger R = longer time Bigger C = longer time
Example with R=100k, C=47μF:
T = 1.1 × 100,000 × 0.000047
T = 5.17 seconds — about 5 sec!

Double the capacitor → time doubles!

💡 My Activity Today — Build the One-Shot Circuit!
+9V ----+----[10k pull-up]----+---- Pin 2 (Trigger) | | [R] 100k [Button] ---> GND (press to trigger!) | +---------- Pin 6 (Threshold) + Pin 7 (Discharge) | [C] 47uF (long leg + to Pin 6/7 side!) | GND Pin 8 (VCC) ---- +9V Pin 1 (GND) ---- GND Pin 4 (Reset) -- +9V Pin 5 (Control) -[0.01uF]- GND Pin 3 (Output) --[330Ω]-- LED (+) --> LED (-) --> GND
When you press the button, Pin 2 drops to GND. The 555 wakes up, turns on the LED, and starts charging the capacitor. When the cap hits 6V (2/3 of 9V), the 555 turns the LED off automatically!
✅ Wiring Steps — Check Each One:
  1. Place 555 IC across the center gap, notch facing UP
  2. Pin 1 (GND) → negative rail  |  Pin 8 (VCC) → positive rail  |  Pin 4 (Reset) → positive rail
  3. 100kΩ resistor: between positive rail and the Pin 7 row
  4. Short jumper wire connecting Pin 6 to Pin 7 (they share the same node)
  5. 47μF capacitor: long leg (+) to Pin 6/7 row, short leg (−) to negative rail
  6. 0.01μF ceramic cap: Pin 5 to negative rail (noise filter)
  7. Trigger: 10kΩ pull-up from positive rail to Pin 2, then push button from Pin 2 to negative rail
  8. LED + 330Ω: Pin 3 → 330Ω → LED (+leg) → LED (−leg) → negative rail
  9. Connect 9V battery. Press button — LED should glow for about 5 seconds then turn off!

🔬 4 Experiments You Did Today!
A
The 5-Second One-Shot
Press the button and let go. Use your stopwatch to time the LED.
LED stays on for about 5 seconds then turns itself off — just like a hallway light!
B
Swap the Resistor (47k)
Replace 100kΩ with 47kΩ and time the pulse again.
Smaller R = shorter time (~2.4 sec). Half the resistance → roughly half the wait!
C
Swap the Capacitor (100μF)
Replace 47μF with 100μF. Time the new pulse duration.
Bigger C = longer time (~11 sec). Bigger bucket takes longer to fill!
D
Wand Check: Watch the Capacitor Charge
Red probe on capacitor (+) leg. Press button and watch the Wand display.
Voltage slowly climbs 0V → 6V (2/3 of 9V), then drops back fast when the timer resets!
