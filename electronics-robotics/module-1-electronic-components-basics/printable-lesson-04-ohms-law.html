📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🔮 =
Ohm's Law
The rule that connects voltage, current, and resistance. One formula to rule them all: V = I x R
🔅 V
Voltage (V)
The push — like water pressure from a pump. More voltage = bigger push = more current flows!
💦 I
Current (I)
The flow — like how much water moves through the pipe every second. Measured in Amps (A).
🚫 R
Resistance (R)
The block — like how narrow the pipe is. More resistance = less current = dimmer LED!
△ The Magic Triangle — V = I x R
V  =  I  ×  R

Voltage = Current × Resistance. This is the most famous formula in electronics! Cover any letter with your finger to find the formula for that value.

V
I
R

with your finger!
V Cover V → V = I × R Voltage = Current times Resistance
I Cover I → I = V ÷ R Current = Voltage ÷ Resistance
R Cover R → R = V ÷ I Resistance = Voltage ÷ Current
Example: 9V battery + 1,000Ω resistor → I = 9 ÷ 1000 = 0.009 A (9 milliamps)
💧 Why it Works — The Water Analogy

Electricity is just like water in a pipe system!

Water System Electrical Circuit What changes
Pump pressure Voltage (V) How hard electricity is pushed
Water flow rate Current (I) How much electricity flows
Narrow pipe Resistance (R) How much the flow is blocked
Voltage pushes current. Resistance blocks current. They work against each other — and Ohm's Law tells you exactly how they balance out!

🔧 Troubleshooting Quick Guide
ProblemQuick Fix
