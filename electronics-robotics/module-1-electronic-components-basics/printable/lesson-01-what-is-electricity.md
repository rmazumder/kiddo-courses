**Age:** 6--12 years | **Time:** 40--45 min | **XP:** 220

## Three Key Ideas

![Water Pump Loop Analogy](../images/lesson-01-water-pump-loop.png)

**Electricity is like water flowing through pipes:**

| Concept | Electricity | Water Analogy |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Voltage (V)** | Electrical pressure | Water pump pressure |
| **Current (I)** | Flow of electrons | Water flowing |
| **Resistance (R)** | Opposition to flow | Narrow pipe section |

---

## Your First Circuit

![Breadboard Circuit](../images/lesson-01-breadboard-first-circuit.png)

**Simple circuit: Battery → Resistor → LED → Ground**

- 🔴 **Red wire** = Positive (power) rail
- 🟢 **Green wire** = Negative/Ground rail
- 🟠 **Resistor** = Protects LED from too much current
- 💡 **LED** = Light emits when current flows

---

## Break and Restore

![Circuit Broken and Restored](../images/lesson-01-break-restore-circuit.png)

**Current needs a complete PATH to flow:**

- Remove ANY wire → Circuit breaks → LED off
- Reconnect wire → Circuit closes → LED on

---

## AC vs DC

![AC DC Comparison](../images/lesson-01-ac-dc-comparison.png)

| Type | Current Direction | Source | Use |
|------|-------------------|--------|-----|
| **DC** | One direction (→) | Batteries | Electronics, toys, robots |
| **AC** | Back and forth (↔) | Wall outlets | House power, appliances |

---

## Real-World Uses

- 🔋 **Batteries** = DC power for flashlights, toys
- ⚡ **Lightning** = Natural electricity (very high voltage!)
- 🏠 **House outlets** = AC power
- 🤖 **Arduino** = Uses DC batteries
- 💡 **LEDs** = Need correct polarity (DC only)

---

## Quick Quiz

**Q1:** What does voltage do?
**A:** It provides the "pressure" to push current through a circuit.

**Q2:** What does a resistor do?
**A:** It slows down current and protects components like LEDs.

**Q3:** What's the difference between AC and DC?
**A:** DC flows one direction (batteries), AC oscillates back and forth (wall outlets).
