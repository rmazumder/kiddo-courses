📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🔅 V
Voltage (V)
How hard the battery "pushes" electricity. More voltage = bigger push! Like water pressure in a pipe.
💦 A
Current (A)
How much electricity is actually flowing. Measured in Amps. Like the amount of water rushing through a pipe.
🚫 Ω
Resistance (Ω)
Something that slows the electricity down. Measured in Ohms. Like a narrow section of pipe that restricts flow.
🔄 ↻
Circuit
A complete loop — electricity must travel in a full circle. Break the loop and everything stops!

⚡ Two Kinds of Electricity
🈀 DC — Direct Current
Voltage | ________________________ | +----------------------- Time
Goes ONE direction, steady and calm — like ants marching in a line! 🀴
Used in: batteries, LEDs, phones, robots, Arduino.
Wall outlet chargers convert AC → DC for your devices!
⚡ Father of DC: Thomas Edison
🌀 AC — Alternating Current
Voltage | /\ /\ /\ | / \ / \ / \ |--/----\--/----\--/----\- Time
Switches direction 60 times per second! Back and forth like ocean waves! 🌊
Found in: wall outlets, home appliances.
Great for sending power long distances across cities!
⚡ Father of AC: Nikola Tesla

💡 My First Circuit Build!
9V Battery (+) ➔ [330Ω Resistor] ➔ LED (+leg) ➔ LED (−leg) ➔ Battery (−) Breadboard wiring: 1. Battery clip: RED wire ➔ (+) rail | BLACK wire ➔ (−) rail 2. Resistor: one leg in (+) rail, other leg in row 5 3. LED: long leg (+ anode) in row 5, short leg (− cathode) in row 7 4. Wire: row 7 ➔ (−) rail 5. Connect battery ➔ LED lights up! 💡
The resistor is like a narrow pipe — it slows the electricity down and protects the LED from getting too much current!

🧠 Quiz Time! Fill in the blanks.
1. A battery ________________ electricity around the circuit.
hint: "pushes"
2. If you remove a wire, the circuit ________________ and the LED goes off.
hint: "breaks"
3. The resistor stops the LED from getting too much ________________.
hint: "current"
🆕 BONUS: The battery is 9V but the LED only needs 2V. Where does the extra 7V go?
hint: "into the resistor, as heat!"
🔬 4 Experiments You Did Today!
A
Break the Circuit
Pull out the resistor.
LED goes off — the loop is broken!
B
Reverse the LED
Flip the LED around.
LEDs have polarity — electricity only flows ONE direction!
C
The Missing Wire
Remove one jumper wire.
Electricity has nowhere to go — the loop is broken!
D
You ARE the Circuit!
Hold both bare wire ends with your fingers.
A tiny, safe current flows through YOU — you completed the loop!
