📚 Key Words — Learn These!
💡 T
Transistor
The most important electronic component ever invented! It acts as a tiny electronic switch. Your phone has 10 billion of them!
👉 B
Base (B)
The control leg of the transistor. A tiny signal here switches the transistor ON or OFF — like turning a faucet handle.
📵 C
Collector (C)
The leg where current enters the transistor from the load (LED, motor, etc.). Connects toward the positive side of the circuit.
🠗 E
Emitter (E)
The leg where current exits the transistor. Connects to GND (ground). Electricity flows C → E when the transistor is ON.

💡 My Activity Today — Transistor Switch Circuit!
9V (+) ➔ [330Ω] ➔ LED(+) ➔ LED(−) ➔ Collector (C) Emitter (E) ➔ GND Base (B) ➔ [10kΩ] ➔ Button ➔ 9V (+) Steps: 1. Place 2N2222 transistor in breadboard (legs in separate rows) 2. Collector ➔ 330Ω resistor ➔ LED(+) | LED(−) ➔ 9V (+) rail 3. Emitter ➔ GND rail 4. Base ➔ 10kΩ resistor ➔ button ➔ 9V (+) rail 5. Press button → LED lights up! ✦ You controlled electricity with electricity!
Two resistors: 330Ω protects the LED. 10kΩ protects the Base — the Base only needs a tiny current, and too much would destroy the transistor!

🔬 Activities & Experiments You Did Today!
A
Build the Transistor Switch
Build the circuit with button, 10kΩ, transistor, 330Ω, and LED.
Without button: LED off. Press button: LED on — transistor magic!
B
Wand Check — Prove the Switch!
Measure voltage across LED with button OFF, then ON. Then measure Base to GND.
0V across LED when OFF, ~2V when ON. Only 0.7V at the Base switches it all!
C
Touch Switch!
Remove the button and replace with two bare wire ends. Touch both wires with your finger.
LED glows dimly! Your skin resistance (~10kΩ) lets a tiny current reach the Base.
D
Skin Resistance Experiment
Touch wires with a dry finger, then a wet finger, then a coin.
Wet finger = brighter LED (lower resistance). Coin = brightest! Remember measuring your finger in Lesson 2?
🔧 Try at Home: Touch Sensor!
Set up the touch-switch circuit at home (two wires instead of a button).

Try: dry finger, wet finger, and a damp cloth touching both wires.

"Which one makes the LED brightest? Why?"

This is the basic idea behind the touchscreen on your phone!
