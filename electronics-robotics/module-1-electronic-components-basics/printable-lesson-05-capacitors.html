📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🌅 C
Capacitor
A component that stores electrical energy and releases it all at once — like a water balloon that fills up, then bursts!
💧 μF
Capacitance
How much energy a capacitor can hold. Bigger capacitance = bigger "water balloon." Measured in Farads (F).
🚫 +/−
Polarity
Having a positive (+) and negative (−) side that must be connected the right way. Put it in backwards and it can overheat or pop!
📊 F
Farad (F)
The unit of capacitance. Most capacitors are tiny fractions — microfarads (μF). 1,000 μF = 0.001 Farad. That is still enough to flash an LED!

🔬 Two Types of Capacitors
🏭 Electrolytic (the big one)
+--+ | | <-- aluminum can | | (-) | | | | long leg = (+) short leg = (-) stripe marks (-) side
Has polarity — must go in the right way!
The stripe on the side marks the negative (−) leg.
Holds much more charge than ceramic.
⚠ Check the stripe before connecting!
🍖 Ceramic (the small disc)
+---+ | | <-- flat disc +---+ any leg = any side no polarity — go wild!
No polarity — either way works fine!
Holds much less charge (0.1 μF vs 1000 μF).
Great for smoothing tiny signal noise.
✓ Safe to insert either way!

💡 My Activity Today — Charge, Flash, and Watch it Drain!
Experiment 1 — Charge a Capacitor & Light an LED: Step 1 — CHARGE: 9V(+) ➔ Capacitor(+leg) | 9V(−) ➔ Capacitor(−leg) Wait 5 seconds. Disconnect battery. Step 2 — DISCHARGE: Cap(+) ➔ [330Ω] ➔ LED(+) ➔ LED(−) ➔ Cap(−) LED flashes! How long did it glow? Experiment 2 — Wand Drain Watch: 1. Charge 1000μF cap from 9V for 5 seconds. Disconnect battery. 2. Set Wand to DC Volts. Touch red probe to (+), black to (−). 3. Connect 10kΩ resistor across the cap legs. Watch the number DROP!
The bigger the capacitor, the longer it glows! 1000 μF glows longer than 100 μF — more "balloon", more charge.

🔌 4 Things You Tried Today!
A
The Magic Flash
Charge a 1000 μF cap, disconnect battery, touch legs to LED.
The LED flashed — the cap stored electricity and gave it to the LED without the battery!
B
Big vs. Small Cap
Repeat with 1000 μF, then 100 μF. Compare the glow time.
The 1000 μF glowed longer — a bigger capacitor holds more charge!
C
Wand Drain Watch
Charge cap, then add 10kΩ resistor and watch Wand voltage drop over 60 seconds.
Voltage drops fast at first, then slower — scientists call this exponential decay!
D
Copy the Battery
Measure the battery voltage. Then measure the fully-charged cap voltage.
Both read ~9V! The capacitor "copied" the battery's voltage perfectly!
