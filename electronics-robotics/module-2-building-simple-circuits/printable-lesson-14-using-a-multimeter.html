📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🔋 V
DC Voltage
The "push" in a circuit, measured in Volts. Connect probes across (in parallel with) the component to measure it. Red probe goes in V/ohm jack.
💦 A
DC Current (Amps)
How much electricity flows through the circuit. Must be measured in series — break the circuit and insert the Wand. Red probe moves to the mA jack!
🚫 Ω
Resistance (Ohms)
How much a component blocks the flow. Always measure with the power OFF. If the Wand reads OL, switch to a higher range — resistance is bigger than your setting!
🔔 ↻
Continuity
A beep test — the Wand beeps if electricity CAN flow through a path. Use it to check wires, find breaks, and trace connections. Always power OFF first!
⚡ The Four Superpowers of Your Magic Measurement Wand
🔋 1. DC Voltage (V ―)
What: Measures electrical "push" in Volts
Red probe jack: V/ohm
Connect: ACROSS the component (parallel)
Think: "How hard is the battery pushing here?"
💦 2. DC Current (A ―)
What: Measures electrical "flow" in Amps or mA
Red probe jack: mA or A (NOT V/ohm!)
Connect: IN SERIES — break circuit, insert Wand
Think: "How much electricity is actually flowing?"
🚫 3. Resistance (Ω horseshoe)
What: Measures how much something blocks flow
Red probe jack: V/ohm
Connect: ACROSS component — POWER OFF first!
Think: "How much does this block electricity?"
🔔 4. Continuity (speaker symbol)
What: BEEPS if electricity can flow through a path
Red probe jack: V/ohm
Connect: ACROSS the path — POWER OFF first!
Think: "Is this path connected or broken?"

🔌 Voltage Drops — The Numbers Always Add Up!
Circuit: 9V battery ----[330Ω resistor]----[LED]---- GND Measurement A: Red on + rail, Black on - rail --------> reads ~9.0 V (full battery) Measurement B: Red on + side of resistor, Black on - side --> reads ~7.0 V (resistor's share) Measurement C: Red on LED anode (+), Black on cathode (-) ---> reads ~2.0 V (LED's share) CHECK: 7V + 2V = 9V <--- Kirchhoff's Voltage Law! The drops always add up to the source!
Connect probes ACROSS (in parallel with) each component — circuit stays powered ON for voltage!

💡 My Activity Today — The Master Challenge!
DEBUGGING CIRCUIT: 9V ---[Resistor]---[LED 1]---[LED 2]--- GND STEP 1 (Voltage mode): Red probe to V/ohm jack. Measure across battery --> should be ~9V STEP 2 (Voltage mode): Measure across each LED and resistor. Note the readings. STEP 3 (Continuity mode): DISCONNECT battery. Test each wire and connection for beep. STEP 4 (Resistance mode): Measure the resistor. Is it really 330Ω or something else? STEP 5 (Fix & retest!): Fix what you found, reconnect battery, and recheck.
After measuring current, ALWAYS move the red probe back to the V/ohm jack — this is the #1 mistake to avoid!

🔬 4 Experiments You Did Today!
A
Voltage Drop Hunt
Measure voltage across the resistor, then the LED, then the battery.
The drops ADD UP to the battery voltage — Kirchhoff's Voltage Law!
B
Current in Series
Move red probe to mA, break the circuit, insert Wand in the gap.
About 20 mA flows — match it with Ohm's Law: I = V/R!
C
Measure Your Body!
Set dial to resistance. Hold one probe in each hand. Grip tighter or dampen hands.
Dry hands: ~1MΩ. Damp hands: much lower — water conducts!
D
Continuity Obstacle Course
With power OFF, trace a breadboard circuit step-by-step using the beep test.
Beep = connected path! Follow the circuit like a treasure map!
