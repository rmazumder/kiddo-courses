📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🛣 ∥
Parallel Circuit
A circuit with multiple paths for electricity to travel. Like a highway with many lanes — if one lane closes, other lanes keep flowing!
💦 A
Branch Current
The amount of current flowing in one path of a parallel circuit. Each branch can carry a different amount of current!
⚡ Σ
Kirchhoff's Current Law
The total current from the battery equals the sum of all branch currents. What goes in must come out!
🔌 A
Ammeter Mode
A special setting on the multimeter to measure current (in Amps or mA). The meter must be wired in series with the branch!
🛣 The Big Idea — Electricity Gets Choices!
In a series circuit, there is only ONE road. If it breaks, everything stops. In a parallel circuit, electricity has MULTIPLE roads to choose from — just like a highway!
SERIES (one road): Battery (+) --[R1]--[R2]--[R3]-- Battery (-) If R2 breaks: EVERYTHING stops! PARALLEL (multiple roads): +--[R1 + RED LED]---+ | | Batt(+) ----+--[R2 + GRN LED]---+---- Batt(-) | | +--[R3 + YLW LED]---+ If R2 breaks: R1 and R3 KEEP WORKING!
Each branch is a separate road from (+) to (-). Break one road — the others don't care!
📋 Series vs. Parallel — Side by Side
The two most important circuit types have opposite superpowers:
Rule Series Circuit Parallel Circuit
Paths ONE path for current MULTIPLE paths for current
Current Same everywhere Splits between branches
Voltage Splits between components Same across ALL branches
One breaks Everything stops Other branches keep working
LED brightness Dim (sharing voltage) Bright (each gets full V)
💡 Kirchhoff's Current Law in Action:
Total current IN: I_total = 60 mA Branch 1 (Red): I_1 = 20 mA Branch 2 (Green): I_2 = 20 mA Branch 3 (Yellow): I_3 = 20 mA Check: 20 + 20 + 20 = 60 mA (it adds up!)

💡 My Activity Today — Build a 3-Branch Parallel Circuit!
Three LEDs wired in PARALLEL on the breadboard: +--[330Ω]--[RED LED (+)(-)]--+ | | 9V(+)--+--[330Ω]--[GREEN LED (+)(-)]--+--9V(-) | | +--[330Ω]--[YELLOW LED(+)(-)]--+ Each LED has its own private road from (+) to (-)!
  • 1Battery clip: RED wire to (+) rail, BLACK wire to (-) rail
  • 2Branch 1: jumper from (+) rail → row 5, 330Ω resistor rows 5→7, RED LED long leg row 7, short leg row 9, jumper row 9 → (-) rail
  • 3Branch 2: jumper from (+) rail → row 12, 330Ω resistor rows 12→14, GREEN LED long leg row 14, short leg row 16, jumper row 16 → (-) rail
  • 4Branch 3: jumper from (+) rail → row 19, 330Ω resistor rows 19→21, YELLOW LED long leg row 21, short leg row 23, jumper row 23 → (-) rail
  • 5Connect battery → all 3 LEDs light up BRIGHT! Now remove the GREEN LED — red and yellow stay on!
Each LED gets the FULL 9V from the battery (minus its own resistor drop). That is why they are brighter than series LEDs!
⚠️ NEW Wand Skill: Ammeter Mode Safety Rules!
  • 1️⃣Move the RED probe to the mA or A hole (not the V/Ω hole!)
  • 2️⃣The Wand must be wired IN SERIES — break the circuit and insert the Wand in the gap
  • 3️⃣NEVER connect the ammeter directly across the battery — it will blow the fuse!
  • 4️⃣Start on the highest range (200mA or 10A) first, then switch down for accuracy
✅ Components I Used:

🧠 Quiz Time! Fill in the blanks.
1. In a parallel circuit, ________________ stays the same across every branch.
hint: "voltage"
2. Three parallel branches carry 10 mA, 15 mA, and 25 mA. Total battery current = _______ mA.
hint: add them all up!
3. When you remove one LED from a 3-LED parallel circuit, the other LEDs ________________.
hint: "stay on — they have their own paths"
🆕 BONUS: Why does adding MORE branches in parallel make TOTAL resistance go DOWN?
hint: "more paths = easier flow = lower resistance"
🔬 4 Experiments You Did Today!
A
Voltage Check — All Branches!
Set Wand to DC Volts, measure across each LED branch.
Every branch shows about 9V — parallel means FULL voltage on every road!
B
Measure Branch Current
Move Wand probe to mA jack, break one branch wire, insert Wand in the gap.
Each branch reads about 20–22 mA. All three add up to ~60 mA total!
C
Independence Test
Pull the GREEN LED out of the breadboard.
Red and yellow stay on! Removing one branch doesn't affect the others at all.
D
Parallel vs. Series Brightness
Build both circuits at the same time and compare LEDs side by side.
Parallel LEDs are MUCH brighter! Each gets the full battery voltage, not shared voltage.
🏠 Try at Home: Parallel Lamp Test!
Look at the lights in your home. Are they wired in series or parallel?

Turn off one lamp — do the other lights in the room go out? No! That proves your home wiring is parallel.
