📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🔅 V
Voltage (V)
How hard the battery "pushes" electricity. Measured in Volts. Like water pressure at the top of a waterslide!
💦 I
Current (I)
How much electricity actually flows. Measured in Amps (A) or milliamps (mA). Like the speed of water rushing down the slide.
🚫 Ω
Resistance (R)
How much the path blocks the flow. Measured in Ohms (Ω). Like a narrow waterslide that slows things down.
🧮 =
Ohm's Law
The superpower formula that links all three: V = I × R. Know any two values and you can always find the third!
⚡ The Superpower Formula — V = I × R
+-------+ / V \ / --------- \ / I x R \ +------------------+ Cover what you WANT to find: Cover V --> I x R = V Cover I --> V / R = I Cover R --> V / I = R Draw this on a sticky note! Keep it at your breadboard.
The VIR Triangle — your cheat code!
The Three Magic Forms: V = I x R (find Voltage) I = V / R (find Current) R = V / I (find Resistance) Example: 9V battery, 1000 ohm resistor: I = 9 / 1000 = 0.009 A = 9 milliamps (mA)
Bigger R = Less I = Dimmer LED!
Use the formula BEFORE you measure — make a PREDICTION, then check with your Wand!
💧 The Water Park Analogy — Electricity is Like a Waterslide!
WATER PARK CIRCUIT ===================================================== Water pump at the top (HIGH PRESSURE) | | (water pressure = VOLTAGE) | v [ Wide waterslide ] -- low resistance, FAST flow | v [ Narrow waterslide ] -- high resistance, SLOW flow | v Pool at the bottom --> water returns to pump (loop!) In electricity: Battery (pump) --> Resistor (narrow slide) --> LED --> back to battery Water pressure = Water flow x Slide narrowness VOLTAGE = CURRENT x RESISTANCE V = I x R
Bigger resistor = narrower slide = less current = dimmer LED. That is Ohm's Law in action!

💡 My Activity Today — The Predict-and-Prove Lab!
Circuit Layout (build this on your breadboard): 9V (+) ---> [RESISTOR] ---> LED (+leg) ---> LED (-leg) ---> 9V (-) Wiring Steps: 1. Battery clip: RED wire --> (+) power rail | BLACK wire --> (-) ground rail 2. Resistor: one leg into (+) rail, other leg into row 5 3. LED: LONG leg (+ anode) into row 5, SHORT leg (- cathode) into row 7 4. Jumper wire: row 7 --> (-) ground rail 5. Connect battery --> LED lights up! Repeat with 3 different resistors and PREDICT before you measure!
Remember: The LED "uses" about 2V. Subtract that first! Resistor voltage = 9V - 2V = 7V. Then use I = V / R.

🧠 Quiz Time! Fill in the blanks.

2. A 9V battery pushes through a 1,000Ω resistor. The current is ________________ milliamps.    (Use I = V / R)

hint: "smaller / dimmer"

hint: "467Ω — use 470Ω!"

🔧 Try at Home: Choose the Right Resistor!
You have a 5V power supply (like a USB port) and an LED that needs 20 mA. The LED uses 2V.

Use Ohm's Law to find the right resistor:
R = (5V - 2V) / 0.020A = _____ Ω

Hint: The answer is a standard resistor value you already own! Check your kit.

🔧 Troubleshooting Quick Guide
ProblemQuick Fix
Wand reads 0V across resistorCheck battery connection; test battery voltage first
Predictions are way offMeasure resistor with Wand in Ohms mode first
LED does not light upCheck LED polarity — long leg is (+)
Voltages don't add up to 9VRed probe on (+) side, black on (-) side
Current calculation hugeDouble-check: I = V / R, not R / V!
