📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🍕 ÷
Voltage Divider
Two resistors that split a battery's voltage into a smaller, usable voltage — like sharing pizza slices!
📋 Vout
Vout (Output Voltage)
The "divided" voltage measured at the point between the two resistors. This is the voltage you use!
🔋 Vin
Vin (Input Voltage)
The full battery voltage going into the divider. The two resistors share this voltage between them.
🎤 ⇅
Potentiometer
A knob-controlled resistor that acts as an adjustable voltage divider — turn the knob to change Vout!
💡 How a Voltage Divider Works

Two resistors sit in series between the battery (+) and ground (0V). The point between them is Vout — the divided voltage. The bigger the bottom resistor (R2), the higher Vout is!

Voltage Divider Circuit: Vin (9V) ---+---[ R1 ]---+---[ R2 ]--- GND (0V) | | 9V Vout <-- measure here! (the divided voltage) Pizza Rule: R2 gets MORE voltage when R2 is BIGGER. Equal R1 = R2? Vout = exactly HALF of Vin!
Vout is always the voltage across R2 — the bottom resistor
✎ The Voltage Divider Formula
🍕 Pizza Analogy
You have 9 pizza slices. Your friend is TWICE as hungry. Friend (R2) gets: 6 slices You (R1) gets: 3 slices Hungrier person (bigger R) = more of the voltage!
The bigger resistor always "eats" more voltage. Equal resistors? Equal shares — exactly half each!
🔬 The Formula
R2 Vout = Vin x ------- R1 + R2 Example: R1=1k, R2=1k, Vin=9V 1000 Vout = 9 x --------- = 4.5V 1000+1000 Equal resistors = HALF voltage!
R2 is bigger? Vout goes up.
R1 is bigger? Vout goes down.

💡 My Activity Today — Build It!
On the breadboard (R1 = 1kΩ, R2 = 1kΩ): 1. Jumper wire: + rail ------> row 3 2. R1 (1kΩ): row 3 ------> row 6 3. R2 (1kΩ): row 6 ------> row 9 4. Jumper wire: row 9 ------> - rail (GND) 5. Vout is at row 6 (junction between R1 and R2) 6. Multimeter red probe: row 6 | black probe: GND

🔬 4 Experiments You Did Today!
A
Equal Resistors — Half Voltage
Build with R1=1k, R2=1k. Measure Vout.
Wand reads ~4.5V — equal sharing means exactly half! Formula wins!
B
Unequal Resistors — Bigger R2
Swap to R1=1k, R2=2.2k. Predict first, then measure.
Vout jumps to ~6.19V — bigger R2 eats more voltage!
C
Big Top, Tiny Bottom
Try R1=10k, R2=1k. Predict Vout first!
Vout drops to only ~0.82V — huge R1 hogs almost all the voltage!
D
Potentiometer Dimmer Switch
Wire potentiometer + LED + 330Ω. Turn the knob slowly.
LED fades from OFF to full brightness — you built a dimmer switch!
