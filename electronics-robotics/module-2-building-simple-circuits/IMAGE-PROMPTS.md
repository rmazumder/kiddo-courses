## Lesson 9: The Breadboard

### Filename: `lesson-09-hotel-tunnels-breadboard.png`

**Place in lesson:** Before the opening analogy that explains how a breadboard's internal connections work.

**Prompt:**
Bright, colorful flat-design illustration for kids ages 6-12. Friendly cartoon style, no dark or scary elements. A breadboard shown as a "hotel with secret tunnels." The top half is a normal top-down view of a breadboard with labeled holes. The bottom half is a magical X-ray cutaway view revealing the hidden connections underneath: horizontal colored tunnels (blue) connect the 5 holes in each row of the main area, while long vertical tunnels (red/orange for the + power rail and green for the - ground rail) run along the side rails. Tiny cartoon electrons ride mine carts through the tunnels. Labels point out: "Each row of 5 = secretly connected!" and "Power rails run the whole length!" and "Center gap = NO connection across!" A cheerful cartoon bellhop robot greets electrons at the hotel entrance. The center dividing channel is clearly marked as a gap with no tunnel crossing it.
