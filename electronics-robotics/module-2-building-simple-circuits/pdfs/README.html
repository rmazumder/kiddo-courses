What This Module Is About

In Module 1, you met all the building blocks of electronics and learned how to use your Magic Measurement Wand. Now it is time to learn the RULES of how electricity behaves — and use those rules to build real, working circuits.

Think of Module 1 as learning the letters. Module 2 is learning to write sentences. By the end of this module, you will build a working lighthouse with a blinking light — and you will know EXACTLY why every part of the circuit works, because you will prove it with your Wand!

Module Goals

By the end of this module, your child will:

  • Understand and navigate a breadboard with confidence
  • Apply Ohm's Law (V = I x R) to predict and verify circuit behavior
  • Build and analyze series circuits and parallel circuits
  • Construct and measure voltage dividers
  • Use all four multimeter modes independently (voltage, current, resistance, continuity)
  • Understand different power sources and how to combine batteries
  • Debug circuits systematically using the multimeter
  • Have built a working lighthouse circuit with a blinking LED

Parent/Instructor Notes

  • Sessions of 35—50 minutes work best for Lessons 9—15. The project lesson (Lesson 16) may take 60—90 minutes or can be split across two sessions.
  • The Wand is used more intensively in Module 2. Encourage the child to reach for the multimeter FIRST when something does not work, rather than asking for help.
  • Ages 6—8: Focus on the hands-on experiments and the "predict then measure" pattern. Skip or simplify the math in Lessons 10 and 13. The Wand measurements are the real learning.
  • Ages 9—12: Encourage them to do the Ohm's Law calculations on paper BEFORE measuring, then compare their prediction to the Wand reading. This builds scientific thinking.
  • Wand Journal: Strongly encourage kids to keep a notebook of every measurement they make. Write down what they measured, what they expected, and what the Wand actually showed. This is how real engineers and scientists work.
  • Current measurement safety: Lesson 12 introduces ammeter mode (measuring current). This requires the multimeter to be wired IN SERIES with the circuit. This is the one mode that can blow the multimeter's fuse if done wrong. Review the safety rules in Lesson 14 BEFORE attempting current measurements, or do Lesson 14 first if your learner needs extra preparation.
  • Take photos or videos of every circuit built — kids love looking back at their progress.
  • Track XP visually — continue the poster or whiteboard from Module 1. Seeing the cumulative score grow from Module 1 into Module 2 is extremely motivating.
