📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🏢 +−
Power Rail
The long highway at the top and bottom of the breadboard. Every hole in a + rail is connected. Every hole in a − rail is connected.
📹 ■
Terminal Strip
The main grid where you plug in components. Holes a–e in the same row share a connection. Holes f–j share another.
📏 //
Center Gap
The empty valley down the middle. Nothing crosses it! Left side (a–e) and right side (f–j) are completely separate.
🚌 →
Bus
A shared electrical highway. Every hole on the same bus is secretly connected. Rows are buses. Power rails are long buses!

Think of it as a hotel: rows are secret tunnels. The gap is a wall no tunnel crosses!
🔴 Power Rails
Long rows at top & bottom marked + and −. All holes across the entire rail are connected. Plug the battery here!
🟢 Terminal Strips
Main grid (a–e left, f–j right). Same row number, same side = connected. This is where resistors and LEDs live.
🔵 Center Gap
The valley down the middle. No electrical connection crosses it. Place IC chips over it so each pin gets its own row!

Breadboard layout: Two LEDs sharing one power source + rail --------- 9V battery (+) - rail --------- 9V battery (-) Row 5: [330Ω resistor] leg A in + rail → leg B in row 5 col a Row 5: LED 1 long leg (+) → row 5 col b (SAME ROW as resistor = connected!) Row 7: LED 1 short leg (-) → row 7 col b Row 7: [jumper wire] row 7 col a → - rail Row 10: [330Ω resistor] leg A in + rail → leg B in row 10 col a Row 10: LED 2 long leg (+) → row 10 col b Row 12: LED 2 short leg (-) → row 12 col b Row 12: [jumper wire] row 12 col a → - rail Connect battery → BOTH LEDs light up! ⚡
  1. 1Battery clip: RED wire to (+) rail — BLACK wire to (−) rail
  2. 2First resistor: one leg in (+) rail, other leg in row 5 col a
  3. 3LED 1: long leg in row 5 col b, short leg in row 7 col b
  4. 4Jumper wire: row 7 col a to (−) rail
  5. 5Repeat steps 2–4 for LED 2 in rows 10 and 12

The resistor and LED's long leg share a row — the breadboard's secret tunnel connects them without any wire!

🧠 Quiz Time! Fill in the blanks.
1. On a breadboard, holes a1, b1, c1, d1, e1 are all ________________ to each other.
hint: "connected"
2. The center gap means that hole E1 and hole F1 are NOT ________________, even though they share row 1.
hint: "connected"
3. A resistor is in hole A5 and an LED's long leg is in hole C5. Are they connected? ________________
hint: "Yes — same row, same side!"
🆕 BONUS: Your Wand DOES NOT beep when testing the (+) rail from end to end. What might be wrong?
hint: "the rail has a break in the middle — add a jumper wire!"
🔬 4 Wand Experiments You Tried Today!
A
Same Row, Same Side
Touch probe 1 to A1 and probe 2 to E1.
BEEP! Holes a–e in row 1 share a secret tunnel!
B
Across the Gap
Touch probe 1 to A1 and probe 2 to F1.
No beep! The gap blocks the tunnel completely.
C
Different Rows
Touch probe 1 to A1 and probe 2 to A2.
No beep! Row 1 and Row 2 are separate buses.
D
Power Rail Highway
Touch probe 1 to the first (+) rail hole, probe 2 to the last (+) rail hole.
BEEP! The entire rail is one long highway!
🏠 Try at Home: Draw the Map!
Draw a blank breadboard grid on paper.

Color every hole that is secretly connected the same color. Use a different color for each group.

Example: row 1 left = red, row 1 right = blue, row 2 left = green, + rail = orange.

This map is your cheat sheet for every circuit you build from now on!
🔧 Troubleshooting Quick Guide
ProblemQuick Fix
Wand won't beep same-row testPress probe firmly into the hole
LED does not light upFlip the LED (long leg = +)
LED still offCheck resistor & LED share a row
One LED works, other doesn'tUse Wand continuity to trace each path
Rail won't beep end-to-endAdd jumper wire across the rail break
Wand beeps when it shouldn'tRemove loose stray wires and retest
