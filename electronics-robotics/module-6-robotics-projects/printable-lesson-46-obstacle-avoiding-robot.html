📚 Key Words — Learn These!
👂 ))
Echolocation
Using sound echoes to find objects — exactly what bats do! Your robot uses the same idea with ultrasonic pulses to see what is ahead.
🔋 ~
Ultrasonic Sensor
The HC-SR04 chip sends a sound pulse and times the echo. Distance = (time x speed of sound) ÷ 2. Robot eyes made of sound!
🔁 ↻
Servo Motor
A motor that turns to an exact angle (0°–180°). Mounted on the robot front, it rotates the sensor left and right to scan for obstacles.
🤖 AI
Algorithm
A step-by-step set of decisions the robot follows. Stop, scan left, scan right, choose the path with more space — that is the algorithm!
🐜 How the Robot "Sees" — Echolocation!
Just like a bat sends out a squeak and hears the echo, the HC-SR04 fires an invisible sound pulse and measures how long the echo takes to bounce back. The further away the wall, the longer the wait!
How Ultrasonic Sensing Works: SENSOR )))))))) ---------> WALL | SENSOR (((((((( <--------- ECHO Time between send and receive = distance! Distance = (time x speed of sound) / 2 ------------------------------------------------------- Arduino code that measures distance: digitalWrite(trigPin, HIGH); // fire pulse delayMicroseconds(10); digitalWrite(trigPin, LOW); long duration = pulseIn(echoPin, HIGH); int distance = duration * 0.034 / 2; // result in cm -------------------------------------------------------
Hold your hand in front of the sensor — watch the cm number shrink as you move closer!
🔭 The Scanning System — Sensor on a Servo
🤖 Hardware Assembly
[HC-SR04] <-- ultrasonic sensor | [Servo] <-- rotates the sensor | =============== <-- robot chassis O O <-- wheels
Mount servo centered on the front of the chassis. Attach the HC-SR04 on the servo horn. At 90° the sensor faces straight forward.
Servo pin 6 → Arduino, Trig pin 11, Echo pin 12.
▶ Avoidance Logic Flow
Measure distance ahead | > 25 cm? --YES--> Drive Forward | NO | STOP! Scan Left & Right | Left > Right? --YES--> Turn Left | NO --------> Turn Right | Both blocked? --YES--> Back up!
The robot makes a decision every loop — this is the beginning of artificial intelligence! Tune dangerDistance (default 25 cm) to change how early it reacts.

Upload this code, place an obstacle in front of the robot, and watch it stop, look around, and choose the best path all by itself!
  • Step 1: Attach servo horn to servo, mount HC-SR04 on the horn using a bracket or cardboard.
  • Step 2: Mount the servo centered on the front of the robot chassis. Sensor faces forward at 90°.
  • Step 3: Wire HC-SR04 (VCC→5V, GND→GND, Trig→pin 11, Echo→pin 12) and Servo (Red→5V, Brown→GND, Signal→pin 6).

🔧 Troubleshooting Quick Guide
ProblemQuick Fix
Reads 0 or 999 cmCheck Trig (pin 11) and Echo (pin 12) wires
Always short distanceSomething blocking sensor face — check mounting
Servo jittersCheck 5V power; servo needs enough current
Crashes into thingsIncrease dangerDistance or reduce driveSpeed
Stuck in cornersIncrease backup time in the "TRAPPED" code block
Wand reads 3.5V on VCCBatteries weak — replace or recharge them
