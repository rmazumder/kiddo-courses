📚 Key Words — Learn These!
🤖 ⚙
Chassis
The skeleton of a robot — the flat plate where all parts mount. Like the frame of a car that holds everything together.
🔄 ▶
Differential Drive
Steering by making each wheel spin at different speeds or directions. No steering wheel needed — just control the two motors!
⚡ M
Motor Driver (L298N)
The brain-to-muscle translator. Arduino sends tiny signals; the L298N sends big power to the motors. Controls speed AND direction.
🔋 V
Voltage Drop
When a component uses up some voltage for itself. The L298N uses 1–2V, so motors get less than the full battery voltage.
🤖 Robot Layout — Top View
Robot Layout (Top View): +-----------------------------+ | | | [Arduino] [L298N] | | | | [AA Batt] [9V Batt] | | | [Left Motor] O O [Right Motor] [Left Wheel] [Right Wheel] | | | [Caster Wheel] | +-----------------------------+ Direction of travel -->
Two power supplies keep motors and Arduino happy — motors get 6V (AA pack), Arduino gets 9V (separate battery)!
⚡ L298N Driver & Differential Drive
⚙ L298N Key Pins
+----------------------------+ | OUT1 OUT2 | OUT3 OUT4 | <- Motors | 12V GND 5V | <- Power | ENA IN1 IN2 IN3 IN4 ENB | <- Arduino +----------------------------+ ENA = Motor A speed (PWM Pin 9) IN1/IN2 = Motor A direction (Pins 2,3) IN3/IN4 = Motor B direction (Pins 4,5) ENB = Motor B speed (PWM Pin 10)
Remember: Remove the ENA & ENB jumpers so Arduino can control speed with PWM!
⚡ Common Ground: Arduino GND & L298N GND must be connected!
🔄 Differential Drive Steering
Both FORWARD = Straight Left FAST, Right SLOW = Turn right Left FWD, Right STOP = Sharp right Left FWD, Right BACK = Spin right Both BACKWARD = Reverse Both STOP = Stop!
Like a kayak — paddle harder on the left and you turn right. Paddle forward on one side and backward on the other to spin in place!
🐥 No steering wheel needed — just two motors!

🔬 Try at Home: Speed Test!
Change the PWM value in your code and watch how it changes your robot's speed:

• Try 100 — slow crawl
• Try 150 — medium pace
• Try 200 — fast!
• Try 255 — maximum!

What is the lowest PWM that still makes the wheels turn? Below that value the motor whines but stalls — that is your robot's stall speed!
🔧 Troubleshooting Quick Guide
ProblemQuick Fix
Motors don't spin at allCheck battery polarity; check ENA/ENB jumpers removed
One motor spins wrong waySwap its two wires at L298N output terminals
Robot curves, not straightNormal — will tune speeds in Lesson 44!
L298N gets very hotCheck for short circuits; normal for heavy use
Arduino resets when motors startPower supplies not separated — check batteries
Wand reads 0V at motorCheck ENA/ENB wiring to Arduino pins 9 & 10
