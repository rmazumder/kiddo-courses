## 🔑 Answer Key

<details>
<summary>🔓 Click to reveal all answers!</summary>

| # | Correct Answer | Explanation |
|---|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **C) `PurpleTiger$EatsPizza99!`** | It's long (24 chars), has uppercase, lowercase, numbers, AND special characters. The passphrase method is the strongest! |
| 2 | **A) Two-Factor Authentication** | 2FA requires two types of verification (like password + phone code) for extra security. |
| 3 | **B) Fake messages designed to trick you** | Phishing uses fake emails/texts/websites that look legitimate to steal your information. |
| 4 | **C) Creates urgency and threatens** | Urgency ("Act NOW or lose your account!") is a classic phishing tactic. Real companies rarely threaten you via email. |
| 5 | **C) Scrambling data so only authorized people can read it** | Encryption turns readable plaintext into unreadable ciphertext using a key. |
| 6 | **C) D** | With a shift of 3: A → D (A + 3 positions = D). |
| 7 | **A) COMPUTER** | Shift back by 3: F→C, R→O, P→M, S→P, X→U, W→T, H→E, U→R = COMPUTER! |
| 8 | **B) Encrypted connection with HTTPS** | The padlock means your connection uses HTTPS — data is encrypted between you and the server. |
| 9 | **B) Manipulating people** | Social engineering tricks humans into revealing information, rather than hacking computers directly. |
| 10 | **B) One hack compromises all accounts** | If you use the same password everywhere and one site gets hacked, attackers can access ALL your accounts! |

</details>
