## 🔑 Answer Key

<details>
<summary>🔓 Click to reveal all answers!</summary>

| # | Correct Answer | Explanation |
|---|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **B) False** | The Internet is the network of connected computers. The Web is a service that runs ON the Internet. |
| 2 | **C) 1969** | ARPANET was created in 1969 by the US Department of Defense. The Web came later in 1989. |
| 3 | **B) Tim Berners-Lee** | Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web in 1989 while working at CERN in Switzerland. |
| 4 | **B) An undersea fiber optic cable** | Physical infrastructure (cables, routers, satellites) is part of the Internet. Websites are part of the Web. |
| 5 | **B) A small piece of data with a label and content** | Packets contain a header (label), payload (data), and checksum (error check). |
| 6 | **B) Multiple routes, faster delivery, easy error recovery** | Breaking data into packets makes the Internet fast, reliable, and efficient! |
| 7 | **B) Reads packet addresses and forwards them** | Routers are like traffic cops — they direct packets toward their destination. |
| 8 | **C) Undersea fiber optic cables** | About 99% of international data travels through cables on the ocean floor! |
| 9 | **B) Wired Ethernet cable** | Wired connections are generally faster and more reliable than WiFi. That's why pro gamers use them! |
| 10 | **B) "LO"** | The first message was supposed to be "LOGIN" but the system crashed after just two letters — "LO"! 😂 |
