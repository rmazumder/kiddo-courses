## Lesson 2: Trend Lines

### Filename: `lesson-02-hiking-trail-trend.png`

**Place in lesson:** Before Step 1: Hook, above the hiking trail analogy.

**Prompt:**
Bright, colorful flat-design illustration for kids ages 10-14. Friendly cartoon style, no dark or scary elements. A mountain landscape with a zigzagging hiking trail going from bottom-left to top-right. A cartoon kid hiker walks up the trail with a backpack. The trail goes up, dips down, goes up higher, dips down, continues upward -- mimicking a price chart. Overlaid on the trail, a bright orange straight line is drawn from the first low point to the last low point, cutting through all the zigzags. This straight line is labeled "TREND LINE - shows the real direction!" Green arrows point upward along the straight line. Small flags are planted at each low point where the trail changes from going down to going up, labeled "Low point 1," "Low point 2," "Low point 3." The sky is bright blue with clouds. A speech bubble from the kid reads: "Even though the trail zigzags, I'm going UP!"

## Lesson 3: Moving Averages

### Filename: `lesson-03-noisy-vs-smooth.png`

**Place in lesson:** Before Step 1: Hook, above the noise problem discussion.

**Prompt:**
Bright, colorful flat-design illustration for kids ages 10-14. Friendly cartoon style, no dark or scary elements. A two-panel comparison. Top panel labeled "RAW PRICE DATA (Noisy!)": a very jagged, zigzaggy blue price line that jumps up and down erratically. A cartoon kid covers their ears with a confused expression, surrounded by "noise" scribble effects. A speech bubble reads "Which way is it going?! I can't tell!" Bottom panel labeled "WITH MOVING AVERAGE (Smooth!)": the same jagged blue price line is shown but now with a smooth orange/gold curved line overlaid on top, gently curving upward. The same cartoon kid now wears noise-cancelling headphones and smiles, with a speech bubble: "NOW I can see the trend -- it's going UP!" The smooth line clearly shows the upward direction that was hidden in the noise. Color coding: blue for raw price, orange/gold for the moving average, green arrows showing the revealed upward trend.
