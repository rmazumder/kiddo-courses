# Lesson 1: Risk Management

**Module:** 4 -- Smart Investing
**Difficulty:** Star-3 Advanced
**Session Time:** 35--45 minutes
**Age:** 10--14 years
**XP Available:** 170 XP

---

## Your Mission Today

Welcome to the most important lesson in the ENTIRE course, Smart Investor! Before you learn WHEN to buy or sell, you need to learn something even more important: **how to protect your money**. The #1 rule of investing is NOT "make as much money as possible." It is "do NOT lose more than you can afford to." Today you learn how to be a risk manager -- the guardian of your treasure!

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Explain what risk management means in simple terms
- Know the #1 rule: never invest more than you can afford to lose
- Understand what diversification means and why it matters
- Create a simple risk plan for a pretend portfolio

## How to Teach This Lesson

### Step 1: Hook -- The Egg Basket Problem (5 min)

**Set up the demonstration:**

Put all 10 tokens into ONE cup.

Say:
> "Imagine these 10 tokens are all of your savings. This cup is your Bitcoin investment. Now watch..."

Dramatically knock the cup over. All 10 tokens scatter.

> "OOPS! You just lost EVERYTHING. That is what happens when you put ALL your money into ONE thing. This is why there is a famous saying in investing:"

> **"Never put all your eggs in one basket!"**

Now split the tokens: 4 in cup 1, 3 in cup 2, 3 in cup 3.

Knock over cup 1.

> "You lost 4 tokens, but you STILL HAVE 6! That is the power of spreading out your risk. This is called **diversification**."

**Award: +15 XP for understanding the egg basket lesson!**

---

### Step 2: The Three Rules of Risk Management (10 min)

Say:
> "Professional investors follow these three golden rules. You should too!"

**Rule #1: Never invest more than you can afford to lose**

> "Before you put a SINGLE dollar into any investment, ask yourself: 'If this money disappeared tomorrow, would I be OK?' If the answer is no, do NOT invest it."

```
  The Risk Check:

  Money you NEED:                    Money you CAN invest:
  +--------------------------+       +------------------+
  | Rent / Food / Bills      |       | Extra savings    |
  | Emergency fund           |       | Money you will   |
  | School expenses          |       |   not need for   |
  | Things you need to live  |       |   a long time    |
  +--------------------------+       +------------------+
       NEVER invest this!                OK to invest
                                         (if you choose)
```

**Rule #2: Diversify -- spread your risk**

> "Do not put all your money into just Bitcoin. Smart investors spread their money across different things."

| Investment Type | Example | Risk Level |
|----------------|---------|------------|
| Savings account | Bank savings | Very low risk |
| Index funds | Stock market basket | Medium risk |
| Bitcoin | Cryptocurrency | High risk |

> "A simple rule for young investors: most of your savings should be in SAFE places. Only a small portion should go into risky investments like Bitcoin."

**Rule #3: Have a plan BEFORE you invest**

> "Never invest on impulse. Always decide BEFORE you put money in:"
> - How much will I invest total?
> - How much am I OK with losing?
> - When will I sell?
> - Will I invest all at once or a little at a time?

**Award: +25 XP for learning the three golden rules!**

---

### Step 3: What Is a Stop Loss? (8 min)

Say:
> "A **stop loss** is like a safety net. It is a price at which you decide IN ADVANCE to sell your investment to prevent further losses."

**The movie ticket analogy:**

> "Imagine you buy a movie ticket for $15. The movie starts and it is TERRIBLE. After 30 minutes, do you sit through the whole thing just because you paid $15? Or do you leave and use the remaining time doing something better?"

> "A stop loss is the same idea. You decide: 'If Bitcoin drops 10% below what I paid, I will sell and protect the rest of my money.' You cut your losses BEFORE they get worse."

```
  Stop Loss Example:

  You buy Bitcoin at: $50,000
  Your stop loss is at: $45,000 (10% below your buy price)

  Price ($)
    |  50,000 | X  <- You bought here
    |  48,000 |  \
    |  46,000 |   \
    |  45,000 |    X  <- Stop loss triggers, you sell here
    |  44,000 |     (You lost $5,000 but saved the rest!)
    |  40,000 |      \
    |  35,000 |       \  <- If you did NOT have a stop loss,
    |  30,000 |        \    you would have lost $20,000!
    +----------+--------> Time
```

> "Without a stop loss, you might watch your investment drop 40% while hoping it will come back. Hope is NOT a strategy! A stop loss makes the decision for you, removing emotion from the equation."

**Key vocabulary:**

| Word | Kid-Friendly Definition |
|------|------------------------|
| **Stop loss** | A pre-set price where you sell to prevent bigger losses |
| **Cut your losses** | Accept a small loss now to avoid a bigger loss later |
| **Risk-reward ratio** | How much you might gain vs. how much you might lose |

**Award: +20 XP for understanding stop losses!**

---

### Step 4: Activity -- Build a Risk Plan (10 min)

Say:
> "Imagine you have $100 to invest (pretend money). Your mission is to create a RISK PLAN."

**Instructions:**

1. **Decide your allocation** (how to split the $100):
   Fill in the blanks:
   ```
   My $100 Investment Plan:
   +----------------------------------+
   | Safe savings (bank):    $____    |
   | Medium risk (stocks):   $____    |
   | High risk (Bitcoin):    $____    |
   | Total:                  $100     |
   +----------------------------------+
   ```

   (Suggested: $50 safe, $30 medium, $20 high risk)

2. **Set your rules for the Bitcoin portion:**
   ```
   My Bitcoin Rules:
   +----------------------------------+
   | Amount invested:        $____    |
   | Stop loss: sell if it drops __% |
   | Take profit: sell if it rises __% |
   | Maximum I am OK losing:  $____   |
   +----------------------------------+
   ```

3. **Answer the safety check:**
   > "If your $20 in Bitcoin went to $0 tomorrow, would you be OK?"
   (If you followed the plan, YES -- because you only invested money you could afford to lose, and you still have $80 in safer places)

**Discussion:**
> "See how having a PLAN takes the emotion out of investing? You already know what you will do if the price goes up OR down. No panic, no greed -- just following your plan."

**Award: +40 XP for creating your risk plan!**

---

### Step 5: Common Mistakes to Avoid (5 min)

Say:
> "Even smart people make these mistakes. Now that you know about them, YOU will not!"

| Mistake | Why It Is Bad | The Fix |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **FOMO** (Fear Of Missing Out) | Buying because "everyone else is" | Stick to YOUR plan |
| **Panic selling** | Selling because the price dropped | Check your stop loss -- if it has not triggered, WAIT |
| **Putting in too much** | Investing money you need for bills or food | Only invest what you can afford to lose |
| **No plan** | Making emotional decisions in the moment | Always write your plan BEFORE investing |
| **Checking price every minute** | Causes stress and emotional decisions | Check once a day or once a week, not every hour |

> "The BEST investors are actually pretty boring. They make a plan, follow it, and do not check prices every five minutes. Patience is a superpower!"

**Award: +15 XP for learning common mistakes!**

---

**Question 1:** "What is the #1 rule of investing?"

**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 2:** "What does diversification mean?"
(Spreading your money across different investments so you do not lose everything if one fails -- "do not put all your eggs in one basket")
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 3:** "What is a stop loss?"
(A pre-set price where you will sell to prevent bigger losses. You decide it BEFORE investing, not in the moment)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Bonus question:**
> "Your friend says: 'I just put my entire savings into a brand new cryptocurrency that some influencer on TikTok recommended!' Name at least 3 things wrong with this."
(1. Putting entire savings in = not diversified. 2. A single cryptocurrency = very high risk. 3. Following an influencer's tip instead of doing research = no plan. 4. Brand new crypto = very risky and could be a scam. 5. No stop loss or risk plan mentioned)
**+25 XP bonus!**
