# Lesson 3: Support and Resistance

**Module:** 2 -- Reading Charts
**Difficulty:** Star-2 Intermediate
**Session Time:** 35--45 minutes
**Age:** 10--14 years
**XP Available:** 170 XP

---

## Your Mission Today

Welcome back, Chart Reader! You can read line charts AND candlestick charts. Today you are going to unlock an incredible superpower: seeing **invisible walls** on a chart. These walls are called **support** and **resistance**, and they are the reason prices keep bouncing at certain levels. Once you can spot them, you will start to see the market like a pro!

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Explain what support and resistance are
- Find support and resistance levels on a chart
- Understand why prices bounce at these levels
- Know what happens when a price "breaks through" a level

## How to Teach This Lesson

### Step 1: Hook -- The Bouncy Ball (5 min)

**If you have a bouncy ball, use it!** If not, describe the experiment:

Say:
> "Imagine you are bouncing a ball in a room. The ball goes up and hits the CEILING -- what happens?"
(It bounces back down.)

> "The ball comes down and hits the FLOOR -- what happens?"
(It bounces back up.)

> "The floor and ceiling are like invisible walls that the ball keeps bouncing between. Now imagine that the ball is Bitcoin's PRICE. The floor is called **support** and the ceiling is called **resistance**."

```
  Resistance (Ceiling) --->  ========================
                                  /\    /\    /\
  Price bouncing                 /  \  /  \  /  \
  between floor                 /    \/    \/    \
  and ceiling
  Support (Floor) ---------->  ========================
```

> "The price bounces between these two invisible walls -- just like a ball bouncing between the floor and ceiling!"

**Award: +15 XP for understanding the bouncy ball analogy!**

---

### Step 2: Support -- The Floor (8 min)

Say:
> "**Support** is a price level where the price tends to STOP FALLING and bounce back UP. Think of it as the floor."

**Why does it work?**

> "When Bitcoin's price drops to a certain level, some people think: 'That is cheap! I want to buy!' All those buyers jumping in creates enough demand to push the price back up. It is like the floor catching the ball."

```
  Support Example:

  Price ($)
    |
    |  45,000 |     \
    |  44,000 |      \        /\
    |  43,000 |       \      /  \      /
    |  42,000 |        \    /    \    /
    |  41,000 |         \  /      \  /
    |  40,000 |  --------\/--------\/------- <- SUPPORT at $40,000
    |         +---+---+---+---+---+---+---> Time
    |          Week1   Week2   Week3
```

> "See how every time the price drops to $40,000, it bounces back up? That is support in action! Buyers keep stepping in at $40,000 saying 'I will buy at this price.'"

**How to find support:**

1. Look for a price level where the price has bounced UP from **at least 2 times**
2. Draw a horizontal line at that level
3. The more times the price bounces off it, the STRONGER the support

**Award: +20 XP for understanding support!**

---

### Step 3: Resistance -- The Ceiling (8 min)

Say:
> "**Resistance** is the opposite of support. It is a price level where the price tends to STOP RISING and bounce back DOWN. Think of it as the ceiling."

**Why does it work?**

> "When Bitcoin's price rises to a certain level, some people think: 'That is expensive enough! I want to sell and take my profit!' All those sellers create enough supply to push the price back down. It is like the ceiling pushing the ball back down."

```
  Resistance Example:

  Price ($)
    |
    |  50,000 |  --------/\--------/\------- <- RESISTANCE at $50,000
    |  49,000 |         /  \      /  \
    |  48,000 |        /    \    /    \
    |  47,000 |       /      \  /      \
    |  46,000 |      /        \/        \
    |  45,000 |     /
    |         +---+---+---+---+---+---+---> Time
```

> "Every time Bitcoin hits $50,000, sellers come in and push it back down. The price keeps knocking on the ceiling but cannot break through!"

**Award: +20 XP for understanding resistance!**

---

### Step 4: Activity -- Find the Invisible Walls (12 min)

Say:
> "Now it is YOUR turn! I will give you price data, and you need to plot it, then find the support and resistance levels."

**The data (2 weeks of daily closing prices):**

| Day | Price | Day | Price |
|-----|-------|-----|-------|
| Day 1 | $43,000 | Day 8 | $44,000 |
| Day 2 | $45,000 | Day 9 | $47,000 |
| Day 3 | $47,000 | Day 10 | $48,000 |
| Day 4 | $48,000 | Day 11 | $46,000 |
| Day 5 | $46,000 | Day 12 | $44,000 |
| Day 6 | $44,000 | Day 13 | $43,000 |
| Day 7 | $43,000 | Day 14 | $45,000 |

**Instructions:**

1. Draw your chart (Y-axis: $41,000 to $50,000, X-axis: Day 1 to Day 14)
2. Plot all the data points and connect them with a line
3. Look for the FLOOR -- where does the price keep bouncing UP from?
4. Look for the CEILING -- where does the price keep bouncing DOWN from?
5. Draw a BLUE horizontal line at the support level
6. Draw a RED horizontal line at the resistance level

**Answers to discuss:**
- **Support** is around $43,000 (the price bounced up from this level on Day 1, Day 7, and Day 13)
- **Resistance** is around $48,000 (the price bounced down from this level on Day 4 and Day 10)

> "You just found the invisible walls! The price is trapped between $43,000 (floor) and $48,000 (ceiling). Traders call this a **range** or **channel**."

**Award: +40 XP for finding support and resistance!**

---

### Step 5: What Happens When a Wall Breaks? (7 min)

Say:
> "Here is the really exciting part. What happens when the ball hits the ceiling SO hard that it BREAKS THROUGH?"

```
  BREAKOUT (Resistance broken):

  Price ($)
    |  55,000 |                    /
    |  53,000 |                   /
    |  51,000 |                  /
    |  50,000 |  ------/\-------X  <- BREAKTHROUGH!
    |  49,000 |       /  \     /
    |  48,000 |      /    \   /
    |  47,000 |     /      \ /
    |  46,000 |    /
    |         +---+---+---+---+----> Time

  "The price broke through resistance -- this is called a BREAKOUT!"
```

> "When the price breaks through resistance, it often keeps going UP. It is like the ball finally punched through the ceiling and flew into the sky!"

**The role-reversal trick:**

> "Here is a mind-blowing fact: when the price breaks ABOVE resistance, that old resistance level often becomes the NEW support level! The ceiling becomes the floor!"

```
  Role Reversal:

  Before:  $50,000 = Resistance (ceiling)
  After breakthrough:  $50,000 = Support (new floor!)

    |  55,000 |                    /\
    |  53,000 |                   /  \  /
    |  51,000 |                  /    \/
    |  50,000 |  ------/\-------X----------- <- Was ceiling, now floor!
    |  48,000 |       /  \     /
    |  46,000 |      /    \   /
    |         +---+---+---+---+---+---> Time
```

> "And the opposite works too: if the price breaks BELOW support (the floor), that old support often becomes new resistance (a new ceiling)."

**Award: +20 XP for understanding breakouts and role reversal!**

---

### Step 6: Quick Quiz (5 min)

**Question 1:** "What is support?"
(A price level where the price tends to stop falling and bounce back up -- like a floor)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 2:** "The price keeps hitting $60,000 and bouncing back down. What is that level called?"
(Resistance -- it is acting like a ceiling that the price cannot break through)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 3:** "What is a breakout?"
(When the price finally breaks through a support or resistance level instead of bouncing off it)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Bonus question:**
> "The price breaks above resistance at $50,000. What might that old resistance level become?"
(New support! The old ceiling becomes the new floor. If the price drops back to $50,000, it might bounce UP from there instead of being pushed DOWN)
**+25 XP bonus!**
