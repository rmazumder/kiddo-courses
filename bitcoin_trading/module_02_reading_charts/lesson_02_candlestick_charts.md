## Your Mission Today

Welcome back, Chart Reader! You already know how to read a basic line chart. But today you are going to level up MASSIVELY. You are going to learn the chart type that professional traders have used for over 200 years: **candlestick charts**. Each "candle" packs FOUR pieces of information into one shape. Once you crack the code, you will never look at prices the same way again!

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Identify the four parts of a candlestick (open, close, high, low)
- Know the difference between a green (bullish) and red (bearish) candle
- Read a candlestick chart and understand what happened during that time period
- Draw your own candlesticks from data

## How to Teach This Lesson

### Step 1: Hook -- The Story of Japanese Rice Traders (5 min)

Say:
> "Did you know that candlestick charts were invented in Japan in the 1700s? A rice trader named Munehisa Homma used them to track rice prices. He became so successful that he was called the 'god of markets!' These charts are over 200 years old, and traders STILL use them today because they work so well."

> "Think of each candlestick like a tiny story. It tells you: where did the price start? Where did it end? How high did it go? How low did it go? All in one little shape!"

**Award: +10 XP for learning the history of candlesticks!**

---

### Step 2: Anatomy of a Candlestick (12 min)

Say:
> "A candlestick has FOUR important numbers and TWO main parts."

**The four numbers:**

| Number | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Open** | The price at the START of the time period |
| **Close** | The price at the END of the time period |
| **High** | The HIGHEST price during the time period |
| **Low** | The LOWEST price during the time period |

**The two parts:**

```
  GREEN Candle (Price went UP)        RED Candle (Price went DOWN)

       |  <- High                          |  <- High
       |                                   |
    +--+--+                             +--+--+
    |     |  <- Body                    |     |  <- Body
    |     |     (filled green)          |     |     (filled red)
    +--+--+                             +--+--+
       |      Close is on TOP              |      Open is on TOP
       |      Open is on BOTTOM            |      Close is on BOTTOM
       |  <- Low                           |  <- Low

  "The price went UP                  "The price went DOWN
   from open to close!"                from open to close!"
```

**The body and wicks:**

> "The fat rectangle in the middle is called the **body**. It shows the distance between the open and close prices."

> "The thin lines above and below the body are called **wicks** (or **shadows**). They show how high and low the price went during the day, even if it did not stay there."

**The birthday candle analogy:**

> "It looks just like a birthday candle! The fat part is the wax (the body), and the thin parts sticking out are the wick. But here, the 'wick' tells you a story about how wild the price got!"

| Part | Name | What It Tells You |
|------|------|-------------------|
| Fat rectangle | **Body** | The range between open and close |
| Thin line on top | **Upper wick** | The highest price reached (but did not stay) |
| Thin line on bottom | **Lower wick** | The lowest price reached (but did not stay) |
| Green color | **Bullish** | Price went UP (close higher than open) |
| Red color | **Bearish** | Price went DOWN (close lower than open) |

**Key vocabulary:**

| Word | Kid-Friendly Definition |
|------|------------------------|
| **Bullish** | The price went UP -- like a bull charging upward with its horns |
| **Bearish** | The price went DOWN -- like a bear swiping its paw downward |
| **Body** | The fat part of the candle -- shows open to close range |
| **Wick/Shadow** | The thin lines -- show the high and low extremes |

**Award: +25 XP for learning candlestick anatomy!**

---

### Step 3: Activity -- Draw Candlesticks from Data (15 min)

Say:
> "Time to draw your OWN candlesticks! I will give you the data, and you draw the candles."

**Set up your chart:**
- Y-axis: Price from $40,000 to $50,000 (go up by $1,000)
- X-axis: Monday through Friday

**The data:**

| Day | Open | High | Low | Close | Color |
|-----|------|------|-----|-------|-------|
| Monday | $42,000 | $44,000 | $41,000 | $43,500 | Green (close > open) |
| Tuesday | $43,500 | $46,000 | $43,000 | $45,000 | Green |
| Wednesday | $45,000 | $46,500 | $43,500 | $44,000 | Red (close < open) |
| Thursday | $44,000 | $45,000 | $42,000 | $42,500 | Red |
| Friday | $42,500 | $47,000 | $42,000 | $46,500 | Green |

**Step-by-step instructions for Monday:**

1. Find Monday on the X-axis
2. Draw a small horizontal line at $42,000 (the open)
3. Draw a small horizontal line at $43,500 (the close)
4. Since close > open, fill the rectangle between them with GREEN
5. Draw a thin line UP from the body to $44,000 (the high)
6. Draw a thin line DOWN from the body to $41,000 (the low)
7. You just drew your first candlestick!

Now draw the rest! (Help as needed.)

**After drawing all five, discuss:**

> "Look at Friday -- it has really long wicks! What does that tell you?"
(The price moved a LOT during the day. It went as low as $42,000 and as high as $47,000, but ended at $46,500. It was a wild day!)

> "Look at Thursday -- it has a small body. What does that tell you?"
(The open and close were close together. Even though the price moved around during the day, it ended not far from where it started.)

**Award: +40 XP for drawing your candlestick chart!**

### Step 4: Reading Candlestick Stories (8 min)

Say:
> "Every candle tells a mini story about the battle between buyers and sellers. Let me show you some common ones."

```
  Big Green Candle:           Big Red Candle:
  "Buyers dominated!          "Sellers dominated!
   Price shot UP!"             Price crashed DOWN!"

    +--+--+                      |
    |     |                   +--+--+
    |     |                   |     |
    |     |                   |     |
    |     |                   |     |
    +--+--+                   +--+--+
                                 |

  Small Body, Long Wicks:     Tiny Body (Doji):
  "Big fight between           "Nobody won today!
   buyers and sellers!"         Price ended where
                                it started."
       |                         |
    +--+--+                      +
       |                         |
       |
```

**Special candle patterns:**

| Pattern | What It Looks Like | What It Means |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Big green candle** | Tall green body, short wicks | Buyers were in total control -- strong upward move |
| **Big red candle** | Tall red body, short wicks | Sellers were in total control -- strong downward move |
| **Doji** | Tiny or no body, wicks on both sides | Neither buyers nor sellers won -- the market is undecided |
| **Hammer** | Small body at top, long lower wick | Price dropped but buyers pushed it back up -- could mean a bounce |

> "Think of it like a tug-of-war. Green candles mean the buyers (bulls) are winning. Red candles mean the sellers (bears) are winning. A doji means it is a tie!"

**Award: +25 XP for reading candlestick stories!**

---

### Step 5: Candlestick vs. Line Chart (5 min)

Say:
> "So why do traders prefer candlesticks over simple line charts?"

```
  Line chart shows:        Candlestick chart shows:
  - Price at one moment    - Open price
                           - Close price
                           - Highest price
                           - Lowest price
                           - Who won (buyers or sellers)

  1 piece of info          5 pieces of info!
```

> "A line chart is like reading a book summary. A candlestick chart is like reading the WHOLE chapter. You get way more detail about what actually happened!"

**Award: +10 XP for understanding the difference!**

---

### Step 6: Quick Quiz (5 min)

**Question 1:** "A candlestick is green. What does that mean?"
(The price went UP -- the close price is higher than the open price. Buyers won!)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 2:** "What do the thin lines (wicks) on a candlestick show?"
(The highest and lowest prices during that time period)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 3:** "A candle has a tiny body but very long wicks. What does that tell you?"
(There was a big fight between buyers and sellers. The price moved a lot during the day but ended close to where it started)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Bonus question:**
> "You see a candlestick with a long lower wick and a small green body at the top. What happened during that day?"
(The price dropped a lot during the day, but buyers stepped in and pushed it back up. It ended higher than it opened. This is called a 'hammer' and can signal that buyers are taking control)
**+30 XP bonus!**
