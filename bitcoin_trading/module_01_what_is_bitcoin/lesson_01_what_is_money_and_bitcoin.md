# Lesson 1: What Is Money and Bitcoin?

**Module:** 1 -- What Is Bitcoin?
**Difficulty:** Star-1 Beginner
**Session Time:** 35--45 minutes
**Age:** 10--14 years
**XP Available:** 150 XP

---

## Your Mission Today

Welcome, Future Investor! Today you are going on a journey through time to discover how **money** was invented, why it matters, and how a mysterious person named Satoshi Nakamoto created something called **Bitcoin** -- a brand new kind of money that lives entirely on the internet. Ready to explore? Let's go!

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Explain what money is and why humans invented it
- Describe three properties that make something "good money"
- Explain what Bitcoin is in your own words
- Understand why Bitcoin is sometimes called "digital gold"

## How to Teach This Lesson

### Step 1: Hook -- The Bartering Problem (5 min)

**Start with a game.** Give the learner 3--5 small items (a toy car, a snack, a pencil, a sticker, a card). Give yourself 3--5 different items.

Say:
> "I want your sticker. But the only thing I have that you might want is this pencil. What if you do not want my pencil? How do we trade?"

Let them struggle with this for a moment. Then introduce more people (siblings, friends, or imaginary traders):

> "What if there are 10 people, and everyone wants different things? This is called the **barter problem** -- and it is the reason humans invented money!"

**Key point:** Without money, trading is really hard because you have to find someone who wants exactly what you have AND has exactly what you want.

**Award: +15 XP for completing the bartering game!**

---

### Step 2: The History of Money (8 min)

Walk through the evolution of money together:

```

  5000 BC        3000 BC        600 BC         1661 AD        2009 AD
  ---------      ---------      ---------      ---------      ---------
  | Shells |  -> | Gold   |  -> | Coins  |  -> | Paper  |  -> |Bitcoin|
  | Beads  |     | Silver |     | (Metal)|     | Money  |     |(Digital)
  ---------      ---------      ---------      ---------      ---------
  "I'll trade    "Everyone      "Stamped by    "Promise on    "Math-based
   you 5 shells   agrees gold    a king =       paper backed   money on
   for that       is valuable"   trustworthy"   by a bank"     the internet"
   fish!"
```

**Ask these questions:**

1. "Why did people stop using seashells?" (Too easy to find, not rare enough)
2. "Why is gold valuable?" (It is rare, shiny, does not rust, everyone agrees it has value)
3. "What is the problem with carrying gold coins everywhere?" (Heavy! Hard to split into tiny pieces for small purchases)

**Three properties of good money:**

| Property | What It Means | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Scarce** | Hard to find or make more of | Gold is rare; you cannot just print more |
| **Durable** | Does not break or rot | Gold lasts forever; apples do not |
| **Divisible** | Can be split into smaller pieces | A dollar splits into 100 cents |

**Award: +20 XP for answering at least 2 of the 3 questions!**

---

### Step 3: Enter Bitcoin (10 min)

Say:
> "Now here is where it gets exciting. In 2009, a mysterious person (or group) using the name **Satoshi Nakamoto** published a paper on the internet. Nobody knows who Satoshi really is -- it is one of the biggest mysteries in technology! Satoshi invented **Bitcoin**."

**What IS Bitcoin?**

> "Bitcoin is **digital money** -- money that exists only on computers and the internet. There are no physical Bitcoin coins or bills. It is like the coins in a video game, except Bitcoin can be used in the real world to buy real things."

**Why is Bitcoin special?**

Draw or show this comparison:

```
Regular Money (US Dollar)              Bitcoin
================================       ================================
Controlled by: The government          Controlled by: Nobody! (Decentralized)
Who makes more: The central bank       Who makes more: Math and computers
                can print as much                     (only 21 million will
                as they want                          EVER exist)
Where it lives: Banks, your wallet     Where it lives: On the internet
Who keeps track: Banks and             Who keeps track: Thousands of
                 governments                           computers around
                                                       the world
```

**The "Digital Gold" analogy:**

> "People call Bitcoin 'digital gold' because it shares the same properties as gold:"

| Property | Gold | Bitcoin |
|----------|------|---------|
| Scarce | Limited amount on Earth | Only 21 million will ever exist |
| Durable | Does not rust or decay | Lives on computers forever |
| Divisible | Can be melted into smaller pieces | Can be split into 0.00000001 BTC |
| Portable | Heavy to carry | Send anywhere instantly via internet |

> "But Bitcoin has a SUPERPOWER that gold does not have: you can send it to anyone, anywhere in the world, in minutes, without needing a bank!"

**Award: +25 XP for understanding why Bitcoin is called digital gold!**

---

### Step 4: Activity -- Design Your Own Currency (10 min)

Say:
> "Now it is YOUR turn to create money! You are going to design your own currency. It can be anything -- KiddoCoins, DragonBucks, StarTokens -- whatever you want!"

**Instructions:**

1. **Name** your currency (be creative!)
2. **Draw** what it looks like (use colored markers)
3. **Answer these questions about your currency:**
   - How much of it exists? (Is it scarce?)
   - What is it made of? (Is it durable?)
   - Can you break it into smaller pieces? (Is it divisible?)
   - What can people buy with it?
   - Who controls how much is made?

4. **Present** your currency -- explain why people should trust it and use it

> "Congratulations -- you just did what Satoshi Nakamoto did! You thought about what makes money work and designed something new."

**Award: +30 XP for designing your own currency!**

---

### Step 5: How Much Is Bitcoin Worth? (5 min)

Say:
> "Here is a mind-blowing fact. When Bitcoin was first created in 2009, it was worth basically nothing. Someone once paid 10,000 Bitcoin for two pizzas! Today, a single Bitcoin is worth tens of thousands of dollars. Those two pizzas would be worth hundreds of millions of dollars today!"

**Key vocabulary:**

| Word | Kid-Friendly Definition |
|------|------------------------|
| **Price** | How much something costs right now |
| **Value** | How much something is actually worth to people |
| **Volatile** | When a price goes up and down a LOT, really fast -- like a wild rollercoaster |

> "Bitcoin's price changes every second of every day. Sometimes it goes way up. Sometimes it crashes way down. That is what makes it exciting AND risky. In this course, you are going to learn how to READ those price changes and make smart decisions!"

**Award: +10 XP for learning about Bitcoin's price history!**

---

### Step 6: Quick Quiz (5 min)

Ask these questions out loud (keep it fun, not like a test):

**Question 1:** "What problem did money solve?"
(The barter problem -- it is hard to trade when people do not want what you have)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 2:** "Name two properties of good money."
(Any two of: scarce, durable, divisible)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 3:** "Why do people call Bitcoin 'digital gold'?"
(Because it is scarce, durable, and divisible -- just like gold, but it lives on the internet)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Bonus question:**
> "How many Bitcoin will ever exist?"
(21 million -- and never more!)
**+20 XP bonus!**
