# Lesson 3: What Is Trading?

**Module:** 1 -- What Is Bitcoin?
**Difficulty:** Star-1 Beginner
**Session Time:** 35--45 minutes
**Age:** 10--14 years
**XP Available:** 150 XP

---

## Your Mission Today

Welcome back, Explorer! You know what money is. You know what Bitcoin is. Now it is time to learn the thing that makes prices go up and down: **TRADING**. Today you will learn how buying and selling works, what BTC/USD means, and you will run your very own trading card simulation. Let's dive in!

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Explain what trading means in simple terms
- Understand what BTC/USD means (a "trading pair")
- Know what makes prices go up and down (supply and demand)
- Complete a trading simulation activity

## How to Teach This Lesson

### Step 1: Hook -- The Rare Card (5 min)

Say:
> "Imagine you have a rare Pokemon card -- maybe a shiny Charizard. Your friend really wants it. How much would they pay? What if THREE friends all wanted it and you only have one? Would the price go up or down?"

Let them answer. (The price goes UP because more people want it!)

> "Now imagine that EVERYONE in school has the same Charizard card. Would it still be worth a lot?"

(No! Because it is not rare anymore.)

> "Congratulations -- you just learned the #1 rule of trading: **supply and demand**. This is the engine that drives EVERY price in the world, including Bitcoin!"

**Award: +15 XP for understanding supply and demand!**

---

### Step 2: Supply and Demand (8 min)

**The Ice Cream Truck Analogy:**

> "Imagine a hot summer day. There is one ice cream truck on your street. Everyone wants ice cream. The truck driver could charge $5, $10, even $20, because people are desperate for ice cream on a hot day! That is **high demand**."

> "Now imagine the SAME hot day, but there are 15 ice cream trucks on your street. Can they all charge $20? No way! They have to lower their prices to compete. That is **high supply**."

```
Supply and Demand:

  High Demand + Low Supply = Price goes UP
  (1 ice cream truck, 50 thirsty kids)

  Low Demand + High Supply = Price goes DOWN
  (15 ice cream trucks, 3 kids)

  This works EXACTLY the same way with Bitcoin!
```

**How this applies to Bitcoin:**

| Situation | What Happens to Bitcoin Price |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Lots of people want to BUY Bitcoin | Price goes UP (high demand) |
| Lots of people want to SELL Bitcoin | Price goes DOWN (low demand) |
| Good news about Bitcoin in the media | More people want to buy = price UP |
| Scary news about Bitcoin | People panic and sell = price DOWN |
| Only 21 million Bitcoin will ever exist | Low supply = helps keep price UP over time |

**Award: +20 XP for understanding supply and demand with Bitcoin!**

---

### Step 3: What Does BTC/USD Mean? (5 min)

Say:
> "When you see Bitcoin's price on the news, you usually see something like: **BTC/USD = $45,000**. What does that actually mean?"

**Breaking it down:**

```
  BTC / USD
   |     |
   |     +-- US Dollars (what you are paying WITH)
   |
   +-- Bitcoin (what you are buying)

  BTC/USD = $45,000 means:
  "One Bitcoin costs 45,000 US Dollars right now"
```

> "This is called a **trading pair**. It just means: 'How much of this thing (USD) do I need to buy that thing (BTC)?'"

**Other examples kids might recognize:**

| Trading Pair | What It Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| BTC/USD | How many dollars for 1 Bitcoin |
| Charizard/Dollars | How many dollars for 1 Charizard card |
| Pizza/Dollars | How many dollars for 1 pizza |

> "The price is NOT fixed. It changes every single second based on how many people are buying vs. selling. That is what makes trading exciting (and tricky)!"

**Key vocabulary:**

| Word | Kid-Friendly Definition |
|------|------------------------|
| **Trading pair** | Two things being exchanged -- the thing you want and the thing you pay with |
| **BTC** | The abbreviation for Bitcoin (like USD is short for US Dollar) |
| **Buy** | You give USD and receive BTC |
| **Sell** | You give BTC and receive USD |
| **Exchange** | A place (usually a website) where people buy and sell Bitcoin |

**Award: +15 XP for understanding trading pairs!**

---

### Step 4: Activity -- Trading Card Simulation (15 min)

Say:
> "Now you are going to experience trading for real! We are going to play the Trading Floor Game."

**Setup:**
1. Create 20 "asset cards" on index cards. Make them in different rarities:
   - 10 cards labeled "Common" (draw a bronze star)
   - 6 cards labeled "Rare" (draw a silver star)
   - 3 cards labeled "Epic" (draw a gold star)
   - 1 card labeled "Legendary" (draw a diamond)

2. Give each player 20 tokens (play money, coins, buttons -- these represent dollars)

3. Shuffle all asset cards face-down in a pile

**Round 1 -- Discovery:**
- Each player draws 5 cards from the pile (free draw)
- Look at what you got. Count how many of each rarity
- The remaining cards go in a "store" pile

**Round 2 -- Trading (3 minutes):**
- Players can buy cards from each other or from the "store"
- The store sells cards at: Common = 1 token, Rare = 3, Epic = 5, Legendary = 10
- Players set their OWN prices when selling to each other
- Track every transaction on paper:
  ```
  Transaction Log:
  | # | Buyer | Seller | Card | Price |
  |---|-------|--------|------|-------|
  | 1 | Me    | Store  | Rare | 3     |
  | 2 | Mom   | Me     | Epic | 7     |
  ```

**Round 3 -- News Event!**
- Announce: "BREAKING NEWS: Legendary cards can now be traded for a real prize!"
- Watch what happens to the Legendary card's price (it should skyrocket!)
- Do another 2 minutes of trading

**Discussion after the game:**
> "What happened to the Legendary card's price after the news? Why?"
> "Did any Common cards become less valuable? Why?"
> "This is EXACTLY what happens in Bitcoin markets when news comes out!"

**Award: +40 XP for completing the trading simulation!**

---

### Step 5: Buyers, Sellers, and the Market (5 min)

Say:
> "In the real world, Bitcoin trading happens on **exchanges** -- websites where millions of people are buying and selling at the same time. Think of it like a massive, worldwide version of the game we just played."

```
How a Trade Happens:

  BUYER                              SELLER
  "I want to buy                     "I want to sell
   1 BTC for $44,000"                 1 BTC for $44,000"
       |                                  |
       +----> EXCHANGE <-----+
              (Match!)
              "Deal! Trade
               completed!"
```

> "The exchange matches buyers with sellers. When a buyer's price matches a seller's price, the trade happens automatically. The LAST price at which a trade happened is what you see as 'the price of Bitcoin.'"

**Important safety message:**

> "Here is something really important: trading real money is something for ADULTS. In this course, we are learning HOW trading works so you understand the world of finance. But you should NEVER trade real money without a parent's knowledge and permission. Smart investors take their time to learn before they risk real money!"

**Award: +15 XP for understanding how exchanges work!**

---

### Step 6: Quick Quiz (5 min)

**Question 1:** "If lots of people want to buy Bitcoin but very few want to sell, what happens to the price?"
(The price goes UP -- high demand, low supply)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 2:** "What does BTC/USD = $50,000 mean?"
(One Bitcoin costs 50,000 US Dollars)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Question 3:** "What is an exchange?"
(A place -- usually a website -- where people buy and sell Bitcoin. It matches buyers with sellers)
**+10 XP for a correct answer!**

**Bonus question:**
> "In our card game, what happened to prices when the 'news event' was announced? Why does the same thing happen with Bitcoin?"
(Prices went up because demand increased. The same thing happens with Bitcoin when good news comes out -- more people want to buy, pushing the price up)
**+15 XP bonus!**
