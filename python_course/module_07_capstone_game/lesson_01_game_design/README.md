## What Is a Text-Based Adventure Game?

A text-based adventure game is a game where:
- The story is told through text (words on the screen)
- The player makes choices by typing commands
- The game responds differently based on what the player does

It is like a "choose your own adventure" book, but on the computer!

## Famous Examples
- Zork (1977) -- one of the first adventure games ever!
- Colossal Cave Adventure (1976) -- THE original text adventure

---

## Planning Your Game

Before you write ANY code, plan these things:

### 1. The Story
- What is the setting? (A castle? A spaceship? A haunted forest?)
- What is the goal? (Find treasure? Escape? Save someone?)
- What is the conflict? (Monsters? Puzzles? A time limit?)

### 2. The Rooms/Locations
- What rooms or areas does the game have?
- How are they connected?
- What is in each room?

### 3. The Items
- What items can the player find?
- How do items help the player?

### 4. The Player
- What stats does the player have? (Health, inventory, score)
